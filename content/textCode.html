In previous elections, eligible voters in some counties could vote early at select polling locations in the days leading up to Election Day. However, given that Governor Newsom ordered that every registered voter will receive a mail-in ballot for the November 3, 2020 general election, voters are encouraged to vote at home and return their ballot either by mail or at a polling place. For more detailed information about your county’s COVID-19 early voting policies, go here: https://www.sos.ca.gov/elections/voting-resources/county-elections-offices/

In the upcoming November 3, 2020 general election, voters DO NOT need to apply to vote-by mail. All registered voters will receive a vote-by-mail ballot in light of the COVID-19 pandemic. Voting by mail is simple: 1. Fill out your ballot. 1. Use a black ballpoint pen. Do not use felt-tip pens. 2. Fully blacken desired ovals. 3. Do not sign or initial on the ballot. 2. Sign the return envelope (in the indicated box) 1. Place completed ballot in the return envelope provided. 2. Write return address on return envelope. 3. Make sure to complete all information on the outside of the return envelope. 3. Return your ballot in time! 1. Mail 1. Your ballot, inside the return envelope, must be postmarked on or before Election Day and received by your county elections office no later than 17 days after Election Day. Postage is prepaid. 2. *If you are not sure that your vote-by-mail ballot will arrive in time if mailed, bring your ballot to any polling location in the state between 7:00 am and 8:00 pm on Election Day (November 3, 2020). 2. Return via drop off 1. Vote-by-mail ballots can be dropped off at any polling location in the state or at a ballot drop-off location in your county anytime from 7:00 am to 8:00 pm on Election Day (November 3, 2020). Find location information for county-specific ballot drop-off locations here: https://www.sos.ca.gov/elections/voting-resources/county-elections-offices/ 3. Authorize someone to return your ballot on your behalf 1. Anyone may return your ballot for you as long as they do not get paid to do so. In order for your ballot to be counted, make sure to fill out the authorization section found on the outside of your ballot envelope.
