To be eligible to vote by mail in Texas, you must be at least one of the following:

  • 65 years or older
  • Disabled
    • The Texas election code defines a disability as a “sickness or physical condition” that prevents a voter from appearing in person without the risk of “injuring the voter’s health.”
    • At the moment, fear of coronavirus exposure alone does not qualify as a disability to allow individuals to vote absentee. We will update this website if this changes
  • Out of the county on Election Day (November 3, 2020) AND during the period of early voting (October 13, 2020 - October 30, 2020)
  • Be confined in jail, but otherwise eligible

If you are eligible to request an absentee ballot, complete the following steps. Your application requesting an absentee ballot must be mailed and RECEIVED by your county Early Voting Clerk by Friday, October 23, 2020 before the close of regular business hours. If you plan to vote with an absentee ballot, send in your application as soon as possible.

  1. Print the Application for Ballot by Mail (ABBM) form in English (PDF) / Spanish (PDF) OR submit an order online and an ABBM will be mailed to you.
  2. Complete Sections 1 through 8.
  3. Sign and Date Section 10.
  4. If you were unable to sign the application and someone witnessed your signature, that person must complete Section 11.
  5. If someone helped you complete the application or mailed the application for you, that person must complete Section 11.
  6. Affix postage.
    1. If you printed the application you must place it in your own envelope and add postage.
    2. If you ordered the application online and it was mailed to you - fold the application in half, moisten top tab, seal and add postage.
  7. Address and mail the completed ABBM to the Early Voting Clerk in your county. You may also fax the application if a fax machine is available in the early voting clerk’s office. You also have the option of submitting a scanned copy of the completed and signed application to the Early Voting Clerk via email. If an ABBM is faxed or emailed, then the original, hard copy of the application MUST be mailed and received by the early voting clerk no later than the 4th business day after the fax/email (October 29 if you emailed/faxed it on October 23).
    1. The Early Voting Clerk is the County Clerk or Elections Administrator for your county
    2. Contact information, including fax numbers if available, and email addresses for the Early Voting Clerks are available on this website.

You can return your absentee ballot to the Voter Registrar in one of two ways:

  1. The first way to turn in your ballot is to mail your ballot to your Voter Registrar. Your ballot must be postmarked by 7 PM on November 3, 2020 and received by your country Voter Registrar Office by 5 PM on November 4, 2020.
  1. You can also return your ballot to your county clerk office during the early voting period (October 13 - October 30) or on Election Day (November 3) with a valid form of ID.
