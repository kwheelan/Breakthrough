General Questions About Voting


A raíz de la pandemia de COVID-19, el gobernador de California, Gavin Newsom, ordenó que todos los residentes de California registrados para votar van a recibir una boleta en el correo. Sin embargo, si usted prefiere votar en persona, habrá casillas disponibles. Encuentre la información acerca de su casilla 2-4 semanas antes de la elección aquí.

Para votar, es necesario registrarse mínimo 15 días antes de una elección. El último día para registrarse para votar en la elección general del 3 de noviembre del 2020 es el 19 de octubre del 2020. Si usted envía su registro por correo, lo debe haber mandado más tardar ese mismo día. Cualquier persona elegible se puede registrar en línea.
Aunque no es ideal, si está tratando de registrarse después del 19 de octubre del 2020, todavía puede votar. Las personas elegibles para votar en California (vea arriba) pueden completar su registro y votar en la oficina de elecciones de su municipio, casilla o centro de votación dentro de 14 días de la elección. These ballots will be counted once the state verifies the voter registration of these voters. Go here for a list of early voting locations where you can complete the Same Day Voter Registration Process. Or, register at your polling place on Election Day.
ALL of the following must be true to be eligible to vote:
  • US citizen and resident of California. More information for military personnel and those living overseas here.
  • 18 years or older on Election Day (November 3, 2020).
  • Not currently in state or federal prison or on parole for the conviction of a felony. More information here.
  • Not currently found mentally incompetent to vote by a court. More information here.

Formerly incarcerated individuals who are no longer on parole, or are now “off paper,” are eligible to vote in California.
In most cases, voters in California don’t need to show a form of identification to a poll worker before voting. However, if you are voting for the first time after registering to vote by mail and did not provide your driver license number, California identification number or the last four digits of your social security number on your registration form, you may be asked to show a form of identification when you go to the polls. If this applies to you, bring identification with you to your polling place or include a copy of it with your vote-by-mail ballot.

Please bring one of the following as a form of ID:
  • Photo identification, such as:
    • Driver’s license or identification card from any other state
    • Passport
    • Employee ID card
    • ID card provided by a commercial establishment
    • Credit or debit card
    • Student ID
    • Health club ID
    • Insurance plan ID
    • Public housing ID
  • OR copy of one of the following documents:
    • Utility bill
    • Recent bank statement
    • Paycheck or government document including a check that shows the voter’s name and address
    • The sample ballot booklet you received from your county elections office
    • Another document sent to you by a government agency

Questions About Vote-by-Mail


This election cycle, vote-by-mail in California is easy and encouraged. Due to the Covid-19 pandemic, all registered California residents will receive a mail-in ballot. However, if voters prefer to vote in person, some polling places will be available.

In the upcoming November 3, 2020 general election, voters DO NOT need to apply to vote-by mail. All registered voters will receive a vote-by-mail ballot in light of the COVID-19 pandemic.

Voting by mail is simple:
  1. Fill out your ballot.
    • Use a black ballpoint pen. Do not use felt-tip pens.
    • Fully blacken desired ovals.
    • Do not sign or initial on the ballot.
  2. Sign the return envelope (in the indicated box).
    • Place completed ballot in the return envelope provided.
    • Write the return address on the return envelope.
    • Make sure to complete all information on the outside of the return envelope.
  3. Return your ballot in time! You have a few options for returning your ballot. If you are not sure that your vote-by-mail ballot will arrive in time if mailed, bring your ballot to any polling location in the state between 7:00 am and 8:00 pm on Election Day (November 3, 2020).
    1. Mail
      • The return envelope with your ballot must be postmarked on or before Election Day (November 3, 2020) and received by your county elections office no later than 17 days after Election Day (November 20, 2020).
      • You do not need to buy a stamp; postage is prepaid.
    2. Return via drop-off
      • Vote-by-mail ballots can be dropped off at any polling location in the state or at a ballot drop-off location in your county anytime from 7:00 am to 8:00 pm on Election Day (November 3, 2020). Find information for county-specific ballot drop-off locations here.
    3. Authorize someone to return your ballot on your behalf
      • Anyone may return your ballot for you as long as they do not get paid to do so. In order for your ballot to be counted, make sure to fill out the authorization section found on the outside of your ballot envelope.

Voters in California can apply for a second ballot if needed. If you never received your vote-by-mail ballot or if you lost, damaged, or made a mistake on your original ballot, contact your county elections offical to be sent a second vote-by-mail ballot.
