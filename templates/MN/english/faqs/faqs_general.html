If you moved from one place to another in the same county in Texas, you need to update your voter registration.

There are several ways to do this.

  1. Change your information online through the Secretary of State’s website here.
  2. Correct your current voter registration certificate on the back and return it to your County Voter Registrar.
  3. Fill out a new voter registration application form and check the “change” box.
  4. When you apply for or change your Texas driver’s license, change your voter information at the same time.

30 days after submitting your change, you will be sent a new voter registration certificate with your updated address.

If you submit your change by October 5, 2020, you are eligible to vote in your new precinct.

If you do not submit your change by October 5, 2020, you may not vote in your new precinct, but you may vote in your former precinct. Contact your County Voter Registrar with questions.

", "

If you moved to another county in Texas, you must re-register by October 5, 2020. Your application to re-register to vote must be delivered either by mail or in person to the County Voter Registrar of your new county. You will receive a new voter registration certificate 30 days after your application is submitted and accepted.

If you are late to register in your new county, you may be able to vote using a “limited” ballot. A “limited” ballot will only allow you to vote on candidates or issues common between your new and old counties.

If you move to a new county in Texas, but you are late to register in your new county, you may be able to vote using a “limited” ballot. A “limited” ballot will only allow you to vote on candidates or issues common between your new and old counties.

You may only vote using a “limited” ballot once you’ve moved to your new address and only during the early voting period (October 13, 2020 - October 30, 2020). This early vote may be cast in person or by mail (if otherwise qualified to vote by mail). You may not vote using a “limited” ballot on Election Day on November 3, 2020.

The following must also be true to cast a “limited” ballot:

  • You are a current registered voter in your former county of residence
  • You would be eligible to vote in your former county of residence on Election Day, if you were still living in that county
  • You have not re-registered in your county, OR, if you have re-registered, the effective date of the new registration will not be effective on or before Election Day.

If you feel you qualify to vote with a “limited” ballot or have questions about voting with a “limited” ballot, we recommend that you contact the Voter Registrar Office in your new county.

If the voter cannot read the languages on the ballot, the interpreter may also help to translate the language on the ballot for the voter in the voting booth.

All voting systems in Texas are required to be accessible to voters with disabilities. All polling locations must be accessible and provide a way for voters with disabilities to cast a secret ballot.

Voters with disabilities may be eligible for several different options when voting. Options include:

  • Using an interpreter at the polls
  • Curbside voting
  • Voting early, in person or by mail
